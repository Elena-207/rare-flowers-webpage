Rarest Flowers in The World

Corpse Flower (Amorphophallus titanum)

The Corpse Flower is renowned for its enormous size and its pungent odor, similar to rotting flesh. Native to the rainforests of Sumatra, this flower blooms infrequently and only for a brief period. It holds the record for the largest unbranched inflorescence in the plant kingdom.

Ghost Orchid (Dendrophylax lindenii)

The Ghost Orchid is an elusive and rare epiphytic orchid found in Florida and Cuba. It lacks leaves and appears ghostly due to its ethereal, white flowers. Its mysterious nature, coupled with its fascinating adaptations, such as its rootless existence, makes it highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.

Middlemist Red (Middlemist camellia)

The Middlemist Red is an extremely rare flowering plant with beautiful pink flowers. Originally from China, it is believed that only two known specimens exist today, one in New Zealand and the other in the United Kingdom. Its scarcity and historical significance make it one of the rarest flowers in the world.
